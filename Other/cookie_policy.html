Cookie Policy

What is a cookie?

A cookie is a small text file that a website or app sends to a user’s device. This text file collects information about user actions on the website. Cookies store helpful information to enhance users’ experiences with the website, and possibly to improve the websites ability to reconnect with them later. Information collected by cookies can include the user’s preferred language, device settings, browsing activities and other useful information.

When does this website place cookies?

This website uses cookies. Any browser visiting this website will receive cookies from it.

What types of cookies does this website use?

This website uses persistent cookies. A persistent cookie helps this website recognize you as an existing user, so it’s easier to load your settings without defining them again. After defining a settings, a persistent cookie stays in your browser and will be read by this website when you return to it.

What are cookies used for?

Cookies can be used to recognize you when you visit this website, remember your preferences, and give you a personalized experience that’s in line with your settings.

What is Do Not Track (DNT)?

DNT is a concept that has been promoted by regulatory agencies such as the U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC), for the Internet industry to develop and implement a mechanism for allowing Internet users to control the tracking of their online activities across websites by using browser settings. The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) has been working with industry groups, Internet browsers, technology companies, and regulators to develop a DNT technology standard. While some progress has been made, it has been slow. No standard has been adopted to this date. As such, this website does not generally respond to “do not track” signals.

Controlling cookies

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences. However, if you limit the ability of websites to set cookies, you may worsen your overall user experience, since it will no longer be personalized to you. It may also stop you from saving customized settings like login information.

What to do if you don’t want cookies to be set or want them to be removed?

If you do not want to receive cookies, you can change your browser settings on your computer or other device you’re using to access this website. If you use this website without changing your browser settings, we’ll assume that you’re happy to receive all cookies on this website. Most browsers also provide functionality that lets you review and erase cookies, including this sites cookies. Please note that some websites will not work properly without cookies.

To find out more about cookies, including how to see what cookies have been set and how to manage and delete them, visit wikipedia.org, www.allaboutcookies.org, or www.aboutcookies.org.
